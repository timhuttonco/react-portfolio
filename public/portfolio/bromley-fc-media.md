### BFCtv

During my various stints at <a href="http://www.bromleyfc.tv/site/" target="_blank" title="Bromley Football Club">Bromley Football Club</a>, my role included filming and editing video content.

This began when we were one of the first non-league clubs to offer video highlights of matches, along with video interviews. Over time, content was developed to give supporters further insight into the club and players, along with providing club sponsors with additional exposure.

The content produced developed, and while continuing to feature video match highlights of every game, it grew to include video interviews with players and staff, promotional content, and insights into training such as making use of a <a href="http://gopro.com/" target="_blank" title="GoPro">GoPro</a> for a feature called <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?list=PLKwtLLzprS5IdVGmKr_SoLyC5XLhugUA5&amp;v=g42qTAlT3R0" target="_blank" title="BEHIND THE SCENES: Keepers Cam">'Keeper Cam'</a>, which involved goalkeeper <a href="https://twitter.com/sebbrown1" target="_blank" title="Seb Brown">Seb Brown</a> wearing the GoPro during a training session to give a "players view" into training. This video can be seen at the top of this article.

This footage was filmed using a variety of equipment, and was edited on software including <a href="http://www.pinnaclesys.com/publicsite/uk/products/studio/" target="_blank" title="Pinnacle Studio">Pinnacle Studio</a>, <a href="https://www.apple.com/uk/ios/imovie/" target="_blank" title="Apple iMovie">Apple iMovie</a> and <a href="http://www.adobe.com/uk/products/premiere.html" target="_blank" title="Adobe Premiere Pro">Adobe Premiere Pro</a>.
